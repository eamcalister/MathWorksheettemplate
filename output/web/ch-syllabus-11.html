    Section Learning Outcomes

    Course Learning Outcomes.

    • Students demonstrate an understanding of functions in multiple ways including graphical representations, algebraic representations, and verbal descriptions.
    • Students will demonstrate understanding of abstract function notation, proficiency working with abstract functions, and understanding functions that are not defined algebraically. This shall include working with affine transformations of functions, definitions of inverses and compositions of functions, and working with functions that are defined geometrically.
    • Students will demonstrate proficiency working with parametrized families of functions. These families shall include linear functions, polynomials, rational functions, exponential functions, logarithmic functions, and trigonometric functions. Transformations, inverses, and compositions of such functions will also be addressed. Skills shall include finding parameter values from data, evaluating functions, solving equations, and selecting which family would best represent a given data set/real world application.

    Program Learning Outcomes.

    • Students will demonstrate competency in Algebra.
    • Students will demonstrate competency in Geometry.
    • Students will produce and deliver effective written and oral presentations of mathematical material and ideas.
    • Students will apply algebraic, geometric, and computational methods in abstract settings.
    • Students will apply algebraic, geometric, and computational methods in applied settings.

    gTPathways.

    This course meets the following gtPathway Student Learning Outcomes: Quantitative Literacy.
    Competency in quantitative literacy represents a student’s ability to use quantifiable information and mathematical analysis to make connections and draw conclusions. Students with strong quantitative literacy skills understand and can create sophisticated arguments supported by quantitative evidence and can clearly communicate those arguments in a variety of formats (using words, tables, graphs, mathematical equations, etc.).
    Students should be able to:
    1. Interpret Information
      1. Explain information presented in mathematical forms (e.g., equations, graphs, diagrams, tables, words).
    2. Represent Information
      1. Convert information into and between various mathematical forms (e.g., equations, graphs, diagrams, tables, words).
    3. Perform Calculations
      1. Solve problems or equations at the appropriate course level.
      2. Use appropriate mathematical notation.
      3. Solve a variety of different problem types that involve a multi-step solution and address the validity of the results.
    4. Apply and Analyze Information
      1. Make use of graphical objects (such as graphs of equations in two or three variables, histograms, scatterplots of bivariate data, geometrical figures, etc.) to supplement a solution to a typical problem at the appropriate level.
      2. Formulate, organize, and articulate solutions to theoretical and application problems at the appropriate course level.
      3. Make judgments based on mathematical analysis appropriate to the course level.
    5. Communicate Using Mathematical Forms
      1. Express mathematical analysis symbolically, graphically, and in written language that clarifies/justifies/summarizes reasoning (may also include oral communication).
    The gtPathways Student Learning Outcomes will be assessed through the following signature assignment(s): Final Exam.
